Let's start this series off with everyone's favorite security topic: passwords. Managing your passwords can be about as fun as a day spent at the DMV, but today's security tip is going to hopefully offer you some much needed help.

The biggest problem with passwords is that in order to be strong they need to be both long and complex (upper and lowercase letters, numbers, special characters). This means that a strong password is inherently also difficult to remember. This means we either weaken our password to the point of being easily remembered, or worse, we write it down somewhere (yes, please throw away that sticky-note taped to your computer).

Luckily for us programmers exist. And amongst programmers are people who were irked by the conundrum of having a password that is unique, strong, and can be recalled as needed all at the same time. Their solution to this problem: password management software.
